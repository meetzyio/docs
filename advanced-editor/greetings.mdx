## What are Greetings?

Greetings are the initial messages your agent speaks when a call begins. Different greeting types are used depending on whether the call is inbound (customer calling you) or outbound (your agent calling the customer).

<Info>
A well-crafted greeting sets the tone for the entire conversation and helps establish trust with callers.
</Info>

## Types of Greetings

### Inbound Greeting

Used when a customer calls your phone number. The agent should:
- Identify the company
- Welcome the caller
- Offer assistance

**Example:**
```
Hello, thank you for calling Acme Corp. My name is Sarah, 
how can I help you today?
```

### Outbound Greeting

Used when your agent initiates a call to a customer. The agent should:
- Introduce themselves
- State the purpose of the call
- Verify they're speaking with the right person

**Example:**
```
Hello, am I speaking with {{customer_name}}? 
Hi, this is Sarah from Acme Corp. I'm calling about 
your recent inquiry regarding our services.
```

## Using Input Parameters

Make greetings personal by including input parameters:

```
Hello {{customer_name}}, this is Sarah from Acme Corp. 
I'm calling about your order {{order_number}} which is 
scheduled for delivery on {{delivery_date}}.
```

<Tip>
Personalized greetings significantly improve customer engagement and call success rates.
</Tip>

## Greeting Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Keep It Brief" icon="hourglass-half">
    Long greetings can frustrate callers. Aim for 2-3 sentences maximum.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Be Professional" icon="user-tie">
    Use professional language that matches your brand voice.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="State Your Purpose" icon="bullseye">
    For outbound calls, quickly explain why you're calling.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Verify Identity" icon="id-card">
    For outbound calls, confirm you're speaking with the right person.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Match the Language" icon="language">
    Ensure your greeting matches the language your agent is configured to speak.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

```

**Outbound:**
```
