The Billy consumption panel provides real-time visibility into your billing consumption across different assistants and time periods. This floating panel appears in the bottom-left corner of your dashboard when billing rules are configured.

<Info>
The Billy panel automatically appears when you have billing rules configured for your account. If you don't see it, contact your administrator to set up billing configuration.
</Info>

## Panel Overview

The Billy consumption panel displays:

- **Real-time consumption data** for the current billing period
- **Cost breakdown by assistant** with detailed metrics
- **Flexible time period filtering** options
- **Billing adjustments** including minimums and corrections

## Using the Panel

### Expanding and Collapsing

<Steps>
<Step title="Locate the Panel">
The panel appears as a small indicator in the bottom-left corner with a green pulse animation when active
</Step>

<Step title="Expand the Panel">
Click the expand button (down arrow) to view detailed consumption data
</Step>

<Step title="Collapse When Needed">
Click the collapse button (up arrow) to minimize the panel while keeping it accessible
</Step>
</Steps>

### Period Filters

The panel offers multiple ways to filter your consumption data:

#### Predefined Periods

Select from automatically generated billing periods:
- Current billing period (default)
- Previous billing periods
- Historical periods based on your billing cycle

#### Custom Date Range

<Steps>
<Step title="Open Filters">
Click the "Filter" section to expand the date range options
</Step>

<Step title="Set Custom Dates">
Use the "Desde" (From) and "Hasta" (To) date inputs to define your range
</Step>

<Step title="Apply Range">
Click "Aplicar" to load data for your custom period
</Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Custom date ranges must have a start date before or equal to the end date. The panel will show an error if dates are invalid.
</Warning>

## Understanding the Data

### Consumption Breakdown

Each assistant shows:

- **Assistant name** and associated costs
- **Usage units** (minutes, calls, etc.) with unit labels
- **Subtotal cost** for the period
- **Minimum charges** when applicable

### Billing Adjustments

The panel displays additional charges:

- **Minimum Applied**: Additional charges to meet minimum billing requirements
- **Corrections**: Adjustments or credits applied to your account

### Total Calculation

The final total includes:
```
Subtotal + Minimum Applied - Correction Amount = Total Cost
```

## Refreshing Data

<Steps>
<Step title="Manual Refresh">
Click the refresh icon (circular arrow) next to the collapse button
</Step>

<Step title="Automatic Updates">
The panel automatically recalculates when you change filter periods
</Step>

<Step title="Loading States">
A spinning refresh icon indicates data is being recalculated
</Step>
</Steps>

## Panel States

### Normal Operation
- Green pulse indicator shows active monitoring
- All consumption data loads successfully

### Error States
- Amber indicator appears when data cannot be loaded
- "No se pudo cargar el consumo" message displays
- Try refreshing or contact support if errors persist

### Loading States
- "Recalculando consumo..." message during data refresh
- Disabled controls prevent multiple simultaneous requests

## Troubleshooting

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Panel Not Visible">
**Possible causes:**
- No billing rules configured
- Insufficient permissions
- Account not set up for billing

**Solutions:**
- Contact your administrator
- Verify billing configuration
- Check account permissions
</Tab>

<Tab title="Data Not Loading">
**Possible causes:**
- Network connectivity issues
- Server temporary unavailable
- Invalid date range

**Solutions:**
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the panel
- Verify your date range is valid
</Tab>

<Tab title="Incorrect Data">
**Possible causes:**
- Cached data showing
- Recent billing changes not reflected
- Time zone differences

**Solutions:**
- Refresh the panel manually
- Clear browser cache
- Verify your time zone settings
</Tab>
</Tabs>

## Best Practices

2. **Period Comparison**: Use different time periods to track usage trends
3. **Assistant Analysis**: Monitor which assistants consume the most resources
4. **Budget Planning**: Use historical data for future budget planning
