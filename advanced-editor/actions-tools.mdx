## Overview

The Actions & Tools section is the powerhouse of your AI agent's operational capabilities. Here you configure what your agent can do during and after calls - from transferring conversations to human agents or SIP systems, to making intelligent API calls, and setting up sophisticated callback protocols.

<Info>
This section transforms your agent from a simple conversationalist into a fully integrated business automation tool that can take real actions and connect with your existing systems.
</Info>

## Call Transfer

Configure how your agent intelligently transfers calls to human agents, departments, or external phone systems when needed.

### Transfer Configuration

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable Transfer Capability">
    Toggle the "Call Transfer" option to activate the transfer functionality for your agent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Transfer Destination">
    Select between traditional phone numbers or SIP trunk endpoints based on your infrastructure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Transfer Logic">
    Define when and how your agent should initiate transfers using smart prompting and multi-language templates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test Transfer Flow">
    Validate that transfers work correctly with your phone system before going live.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Transfer Types

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Phone Number">
    **Traditional Phone Transfer**
    
    Perfect for transferring to mobile phones, landlines, or traditional business numbers.

    - **Format**: Use international format (+1234567890)
    - **Best for**: Human agents, customer service departments, sales teams
    - **Reliability**: High compatibility with all phone systems
    - **Cost**: Standard telephony rates apply

    **Configuration Example:**
    ```
    Transfer Number: +1-555-123-4567
    Department: Sales Team
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="SIP Trunk">
    **SIP/VoIP Transfer**
    
    Ideal for enterprise environments with PBX systems, call centers, or VoIP infrastructure.

    - **Format**: `sip:extension@gateway_ip:port`
    - **Best for**: Call centers, PBX extensions, enterprise VoIP
    - **Benefits**: Lower costs, advanced routing, better integration
    - **Requirements**: SIP-compatible phone system

    **Configuration Examples:**
    ```
    Sales Extension: sip:100@10.0.1.50:5060
    Support Queue: sip:support@pbx.company.com:5060  
    Manager Direct: sip:200@192.168.1.100:5060
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Transfer v2 (Beta): Multiple Transfer Rules

<Info>
**Transfer v2** is a Beta upgrade to Call Transfer that replaces the single destination model with a flexible list of **transfer rules**. Instead of sending every transfer to the same number, your agent evaluates each rule's condition in real time and routes the call to the matching destination.
</Info>

Enable Transfer v2 with the **"Transfer to multiple destinations (Beta)"** toggle inside the Call Transfer panel. Once active, you can define as many rules as you need.

Each rule includes:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Condition** | A natural-language description of when this rule should trigger (e.g., "the customer asks to speak with sales", "the customer wants to cancel a subscription") |
| **Destination Type** | `Phone` or `SIP`, matching the same formats used in standard Call Transfer |
| **Destination Value** | The phone number (`+1234567890`) or SIP URI (`sip:extension@ip:port`) to route to |
| **Transfer Type** | `Blind` or `Conference` — controls how the handoff is performed |
| **Custom Message** | An optional per-rule message the agent says right before transferring, overriding the default transfer phrase |

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Blind Transfer">
    **Blind (Cold) Transfer**
    
    The agent immediately connects the caller to the destination without staying on the line.

    - **Best for**: Simple routing to a known extension or department
    - **Experience**: Fastest handoff, no context relay
    - **Use when**: The destination doesn't need a live briefing before speaking with the customer
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Conference Transfer">
    **Conference (Warm) Transfer**
    
    The agent stays connected momentarily to introduce the call and pass context before disconnecting.

    - **Best for**: Complex cases, VIP customers, or situations requiring a smooth handoff
    - **Experience**: The receiving agent gets a short summary before taking over
    - **Use when**: Context preservation is critical to avoid the customer repeating themselves
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable Transfer v2">
    Toggle "Transfer to multiple destinations (Beta)" within the Call Transfer section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a Rule">
    Click "Add rule" and describe the condition that should trigger this specific transfer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Destination">
    Choose Phone or SIP and enter the corresponding destination value.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Transfer Type">
    Select Blind for an immediate handoff or Conference to relay context before disconnecting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a Custom Message (Optional)">
    Write a message specific to this rule, such as "Let me connect you with our billing team" for a billing-related rule.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Repeat for Additional Rules">
    Add as many rules as needed to cover all the transfer scenarios for your agent.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Rules are evaluated in the order they appear. Place more specific conditions above general ones to avoid a broad rule capturing calls meant for a more precise destination.
</Warning>

**Example Configuration:**

```
Rule 1
Condition: the customer wants to talk to sales or ask about pricing
Destination: Phone → +1-555-200-1000
Type: Conference
Message: "I'll connect you with our sales team, one moment please."

Rule 2
Condition: the customer reports a technical issue or outage
Destination: SIP → sip:support@pbx.company.com:5060
Type: Blind
Message: "Transferring you to technical support now."

Rule 3
Condition: the customer explicitly asks for a manager or wants to file a complaint
Destination: Phone → +1-555-200-2000
Type: Conference
Message: (default transfer phrase)
```

<Info>
When Transfer v2 is disabled, your agent falls back to the classic single-destination Call Transfer configuration described above.
</Info>

## Webhook Tools

Create powerful API integrations that allow your agent to interact with external systems during conversations through our sophisticated modal interface.

<Info>
**Enhanced Modal Interface**: Webhook Tools now feature an advanced configuration modal with real-time testing, parameter mapping, authentication methods, and intelligent conversation data extraction.
</Info>

### Webhook Tool Modal Interface

Configure webhook tools through our comprehensive modal interface:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tool Identity Configuration">
    Set up the basic tool identity and activation conditions.
    
    **Required Fields:**
    - **Name**: Unique identifier (e.g., "create_lead", "check_inventory")
    - **Description**: Clear explanation of when the AI should use this tool
    - **Execution Mode**: Choose immediate or deferred execution
  </Step>
  <Step title="API Endpoint Setup">
    Configure the core API connection details.
    
    **Configuration Options:**
    - **Base URL**: Complete webhook endpoint URL
    - **HTTP Method**: GET, POST, PUT, PATCH, or DELETE
    - **Request Timeout**: 5-60 seconds (default: 20)
    - **Error Handling**: Retry logic and fallback behavior
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authentication Configuration">
    Set up secure authentication for your API endpoints.
    
    **Supported Authentication Types:**
    - Bearer Token authentication (OAuth 2.0, JWT)
    - API Key authentication (Header or Query parameter)
    - Basic Auth with username/password
    - Custom header authentication
  </Step>
  <Step title="Parameter Mapping">
    Define intelligent parameter mapping with conversation data extraction.
    
    **Parameter Configuration:**
    - **Query Parameters**: URL-encoded for GET requests
    - **Body Parameters**: JSON payload for POST/PUT/PATCH
    - **Header Parameters**: Custom HTTP headers
    - **Value Sources**: LLM extraction, static values, conversation metadata
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Advanced Parameter Configuration

Each webhook parameter supports sophisticated data mapping:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Parameter Properties">
    | Property | Description | Options | Example |
    |----------|-------------|---------|---------|
    | **Name** | Parameter identifier | Custom string | `customer_name` |
    | **Type** | Data validation | string, number, boolean, integer, array, object | `string` |
    | **Required** | Mandatory field | true/false | `true` |
    | **Description** | AI understanding context | Natural language | "Customer's full name from conversation" |
    | **Value Source** | Data extraction method | LLM extraction, static value, metadata | LLM prompt |
    | **Extraction Prompt** | AI instruction | Natural language | "Extract the customer's email address" |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Value Sources">
    **LLM Extraction**
    
    Intelligent conversation data extraction:
    ```json
    {
      "extraction_prompt": "Extract the customer's phone number from the conversation",
      "parameter_type": "string",
      "required": true
    }
    ```

    **Static Values**
    
    Fixed values for consistent data:
    ```json
    {
      "static_value": "phone_call",
      "parameter": "source",
      "type": "string"
    }
    ```

    **Conversation Metadata**
    
    Built-in conversation data:
    - Call duration and timestamps
    - Customer and agent phone numbers
    - Call direction (inbound/outbound)
    - Call status and outcome
    - Agent ID and configuration details
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Data Types">
    **Supported Parameter Types:**

    - **string**: Text data, names, descriptions, addresses
    - **number**: Numeric values with decimal precision
    - **integer**: Whole numbers only (IDs, quantities)
    - **boolean**: true/false values for flags
    - **array**: List of values or complex objects
    - **object**: Nested data structures with properties

    **Type Validation:**
    Automatic validation ensures data consistency and provides clear error messages for type mismatches.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Schedule Controller (Beta)

<Info>
**Schedule Controller** is a new built-in tool (Beta) that lets your agent automatically resolve whether the current call is happening within your configured business hours — no webhook or external calendar needed. It replaces manual date/time reasoning with a structured weekly schedule, holiday calendar, and time zone awareness.
</Info>

Instead of prompting the model to reason about dates, weekdays, and time zones, the Schedule Controller evaluates the call timestamp against your configuration and returns whether the business is **open** or **closed** at that exact moment — including support for holiday exceptions and dedicated messaging for each state.

### What It Solves

- Avoids transferring calls to a human team that isn't available
- Lets the agent give accurate opening hours or a "we're currently closed" response automatically
- Enables conditional logic such as "only offer live transfer during business hours, otherwise offer a callback"
- Accounts for holidays and special closures without extra prompt engineering
- Removes the need for manual date/time reasoning in the system prompt

### Configuration

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable Schedule Controller">
    Add the Schedule Controller tool from the Actions & Tools panel and toggle it on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Your Weekly Schedule">
    Configure opening and closing times independently for each day of the week (Monday through Sunday). Mark any day as fully closed when it doesn't apply — for example, weekends or days your business doesn't operate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Holidays">
    Define specific dates — national holidays, company closures, or one-off exceptions — that should always be treated as closed (or with custom hours) regardless of the regular weekly schedule.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Time Zone">
    Select the time zone your business hours are defined in, so the tool correctly resolves the call time regardless of where the customer is calling from.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set In-Hours and Out-of-Hours Messages">
    Write a custom message the agent uses when the call falls within business hours, and a different message for when it falls outside business hours (including holidays).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reference the Tool in Your Prompt or Rules">
    Use the tool's result in your system prompt logic, or combine it with a Transfer v2 rule condition (e.g., "only transfer to sales if within business hours").
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Weekly Schedule Fields

Each day of the week supports its own independent configuration:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Day** | Monday through Sunday |
| **Status** | Open or Closed for that day |
| **Opening Time** | Start of business hours (e.g., `09:00`) |
| **Closing Time** | End of business hours (e.g., `18:00`) |

### Holidays

Holidays override the regular weekly schedule for a specific date:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Date** | The specific calendar date the holiday applies to |
| **Label** | A descriptive name (e.g., "New Year's Day", "Company Retreat") |
| **Status** | Closed all day, or Open with custom hours for that date |

### In-Hours vs Out-of-Hours Messages

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="In-Hours Message">
    **Used while the business is open**

    Optional message the agent can use to confirm availability, such as:

    ```
    "We're currently open — I can connect you with our team right away."
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Out-of-Hours Message">
    **Used while the business is closed (including holidays)**

    Message explaining unavailability and next steps, such as:

    ```
    "We're currently closed. Our business hours are Monday to Friday, 9 AM to 6 PM. Would you like me to schedule a callback for the next business day?"
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Example Schedule

```
Monday - Friday: 09:00 - 18:00
Saturday: 10:00 - 14:00
Sunday: Closed
Time Zone: Europe/Madrid

Holidays:
- Jan 1: New Year's Day (Closed)
- Dec 25: Christmas Day (Closed)
- Aug 15: Summer Break (Closed)

In-Hours Message: "We're currently open — I can connect you with our team right away."
Out-of-Hours Message: "We're currently closed. Our business hours are Monday to Friday, 9 AM to 6 PM. Would you like me to schedule a callback?"
```

### Example Usage

**Combined with Transfer v2:**

```
Rule 1
Condition: the customer wants to talk to sales AND the schedule is currently open
Destination: Phone → +1-555-200-1000
Type: Conference

Rule 2
Condition: the customer wants to talk to sales AND the schedule is currently closed
Destination: (no transfer — offer to schedule a callback instead)
```

**Combined with End Call Strategy or system prompt:**

Instruct the agent to check the Schedule Controller result before offering a live transfer, and fall back to taking a message or booking a callback when the result is `closed`.

<Warning>
Schedule Controller is currently in **Beta**. Double-check the resolved status during testing, especially around daylight saving time transitions and newly added holidays.
</Warning>

## Recall Protocol

Set up sophisticated automatic callback systems for comprehensive customer follow-up with intelligent scheduling and success detection.

<Info>
**Smart Recall System**: Intelligent callback scheduling that recognizes successful calls, respects customer preferences, and optimizes timing for maximum effectiveness while maintaining regulatory compliance.
</Info>

### Recall System Configuration

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable Recall Protocol">
    Activate the recall system and configure maximum callback attempts (typically 3-5 attempts).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define Success Conditions">
    Specify what constitutes a successful call that doesn't require follow-up:
    - Objective achievement (information gathered, appointment scheduled)
    - Explicit customer requests (no contact, different method preferred)
    - Call completion scenarios (full conversation, clear decline)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Callback Schedule">
    Set intelligent timing intervals between recall attempts:
    - Progressive delays (15 minutes → 4 hours → next day → weekly)
    - Business hours only restrictions
    - Timezone-aware scheduling
    - Holiday and blackout date exclusions
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Escalation Rules">
    Define how callback strategy evolves:
    - Message variation with each attempt
    - Channel switching (phone → email → SMS)
    - Human agent escalation for persistent cases
    - Automatic compliance monitoring
  </Step>
